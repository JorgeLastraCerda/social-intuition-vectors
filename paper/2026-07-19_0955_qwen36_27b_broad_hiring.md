## Result

The broad-strength native-HF run completed all 660 atomic work units and produced 600 validated rows over strengths from -0.50 to +0.50. It took 118.8 seconds on one NVIDIA H100 80GB HBM3 and peaked at 51.14 GiB allocated VRAM. Revision, hook parity, hidden-state parity, single-device placement, text-only execution, and no-TransformerLens gates all passed.

Warmth remained monotone over the broader intervention range. At +0.50, its mean callback-margin change was +2.240 (95% bootstrap CI [2.208, 2.273]); the fitted slope was 5.688 with R-squared 0.938. Competence was also monotone and positive-ended, with a +0.50 effect of +1.069 (95% CI [1.037, 1.102]), slope 4.873, and R-squared 0.917.

The response is therefore robust in sign and ordering beyond the local range, although it is not linear in intervention strength and the callback margins retain the known 0.125-grid quantization. The immediately launched neutral-corpus extraction will support a denoised-vector replication before the conditional 282-name gate is evaluated.
