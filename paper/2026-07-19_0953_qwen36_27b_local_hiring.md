## Result

The native-HF run completed all 660 atomic work units and published 600 raw rows for 60 names, two axes, and five strengths. Local validation confirmed the pinned model revision, a passive-hook logit difference of zero, exact hidden-state agreement, zero vision-forward calls, and no TransformerLens import. Execution took 119.5 seconds on one NVIDIA H100 80GB HBM3 and peaked at 51.14 GiB allocated VRAM.

Warmth steering was monotone across strengths from -0.10 to +0.10. The +0.10 endpoint increased the callback margin by 1.196 on average (95% bootstrap CI [1.171, 1.219]); its fitted slope was 12.142 with R-squared 0.999. Competence was also monotone, with a +0.10 endpoint effect of 0.533 (95% CI [0.506, 0.560]) and slope 7.463 with R-squared 0.973.

The callback-margin diagnostic found all values on the 0.125 grid and only nine unique baseline values among 60 names, so the summary correctly raises a quantization warning. The causal direction and monotonicity are nevertheless unambiguous at this protocol resolution. Broad-strength and denoised-vector runs remain necessary to test robustness beyond the local raw-vector regime.
