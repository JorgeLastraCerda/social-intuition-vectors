# Figure 3 — Steering the warmth/competence direction causally moves the model's social judgement across the Yes/No decision boundary

**File:** `paper_figure3_diverging_steering.{png,pdf}`

---

## One-sentence summary

In all four model × axis combinations, adding ±0.10 × mean residual norm of the concept direction to the residual stream is sufficient to flip the model's explicit Yes/No social judgement from one side of the decision boundary to the other.

---

## Caption (paper-ready)

> **Figure 3.** Steering the warmth/competence direction causally moves the model's explicit social judgement across the Yes/No decision boundary. For each model × axis, the bull's-eye marker (●) shows the unsteered baseline Yes/No logit margin; the coloured bar spans the margin obtained when the concept direction (raw dense) is injected at steering strength −0.10 to +0.10 (as a fraction of the mean residual-stream norm at the probe layer), with the arrowhead at +0.10. The vertical dashed line at x = 0 is the decision boundary; values to the right favour "Yes", values to the left favour "No". In all four cases the steerable range crosses the boundary — i.e. ±0.10 steering is sufficient to flip the model's answer. Gemma-3-12B and Gemma-3-27B; raw dense concept direction; local steering regime (R² ≥ 0.83 across all fits). **Note: this measures concept judgement ("Is the protagonist high in warmth/competence?"), not hiring callback decisions.**

## How the figure was produced

The model receives a short story and is asked: *"Read the story below. Is the protagonist high in warmth, meaning warm, caring, considerate, and well-intentioned? Answer only Yes or No."* (Competence uses an analogous prompt.) The Yes/No logit margin is measured at five steering strengths (−0.10, −0.05, 0, +0.05, +0.10). A local linear regression is fit to these five points; the slope and intercept are stored in `gemma_scope_local_steering_slopes.csv`. The figure plots `intercept ± slope × 0.10` as the bar endpoints and `intercept` as the baseline dot. Steering direction: `raw_dense` (the dense concept direction extracted directly from residual-stream activations). Steering is applied at the probe layer (layer fraction ≈ 0.66). Generated by `paper/figures/generate_figures.py` → `paper_figure3_diverging_steering()`.

## Caveats

1. **Concept judgement, not hiring.** This figure shows that steering shifts the model's answer to "Is the protagonist warm/competent?" — it does not show any effect on hiring callback decisions. That is Phase 6 (future work).
2. **Gemma family only.** Steering data for Qwen3-14B and Llama-3.1-8B is not yet available.
3. **Local regime.** The linear fit is accurate within ±0.10 (R² ≥ 0.83 for all rows). Do not extrapolate beyond this range.
4. **raw_dense direction only.** Other directions (axis-specific, shared, SAE-reconstructed, other-axis, random) are omitted for clarity; they are shown in `fig10_gemma_scope_steering.{png,pdf}`.
5. **Asymmetry is informative.** The baseline (●) is not at the centre of the bar. The asymmetry encodes the model's prior lean — e.g. Gemma-3-12B · Warmth starts at +0.56 (already leaning "Yes"), so the bar extends much further right (+3.34) than left (−2.21).
