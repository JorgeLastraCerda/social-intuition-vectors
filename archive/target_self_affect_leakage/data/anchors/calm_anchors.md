# Calm Anchor Sentences

Sentences used to define the "calm" pole of the embedding projection axis.
These are short, affect-dense sentences representing peace, safety, resolution, and serenity.

Each sentence is listed on its own line. build_anchors.py embeds the full set and saves a mean vector.

---

The morning light came through the curtains and everything felt, for once, entirely still.
He sat by the river and let the sound of the water replace everything else.
She put down the letter, breathed deeply, and felt the knot in her chest dissolve.
The war had ended and the fields were quiet in a way they had not been for years.
He slept through the night without waking, and woke into an ordinary morning.
The children played in the yard and the sound of it was the most reassuring thing in the world.
She had been afraid for so long that the absence of fear felt strange and wonderful.
The doctor said the wound was clean and would heal completely.
He looked out at the harbor and felt, for the first time in months, that the world was manageable.
They sat around the table and no one needed to explain anything; they were home.
The letter from his brother said everyone was well, and he read it three times.
She allowed herself, finally, to believe that the worst was over.
The village was intact, the people were alive, and the road ahead was open.
He had made it through; the realization arrived quietly, like a change in weather.
There was enough food, enough warmth, and enough time — a luxury he had not allowed himself to notice before.
The ceasefire held through the night and the silence was not threatening but restful.
She folded the map, no longer needed, and felt the pleasure of a journey completed.
The trees were in bloom and it seemed impossible that anything terrible had ever happened here.
He counted his losses and then, deliberately, counted what remained — and it was enough.
The world had not ended; it had simply changed, and change could be survived.
