# Fear Anchor Sentences

Sentences used to define the "fear" pole of the embedding projection axis.
These are short, affect-dense sentences drawn from public-domain literary and historical sources,
representing high-fear / despair / dread states.

Each sentence is listed on its own line. build_anchors.py embeds the full set and saves a mean vector.

---

The shell holes were filled with black water and the smell of death hung over everything.
He trembled in the dark, certain that each heartbeat would be his last.
There was no escape, no way forward, and no way back — only the certainty of annihilation.
She heard the artillery in the distance and felt her stomach turn to ice.
The man next to him was screaming, and then suddenly he was not screaming anymore.
Fear had become the only honest feeling left; everything else was performance.
He lay in the mud and counted seconds, waiting for a bullet that felt inevitable.
The world had contracted to the trench wall, the smell of blood, and the sound of his own breathing.
She understood, finally, that no one was coming to save them.
The telegram arrived and everything she had been trying not to think about became real.
He wanted to cry but the cold had taken even that from him.
Nothing moves faster than despair when it has nothing left to lose.
The order came down and the men looked at each other without speaking.
He could not remember what safety felt like; the memory had been eaten by months of terror.
The darkness outside was not empty — it was full of things waiting to happen.
She stared at the wall and felt the slow collapse of everything she had believed.
There is a specific silence that follows catastrophe, and he knew it well by now.
He had stopped praying when he realized the prayers were not reducing the odds.
The survivors moved through the rubble with the careful steps of people who no longer trusted the ground.
Her hands shook even when she was not afraid, because her body had forgotten how not to be.
