# Robustness Audit — Potential Artifacts, Errors, and Unproven Claims

Purpose: list every place in the current pipeline where the paper risks an
artifact, an overclaim, or insufficient robustness for peer review, with a
concrete fix for each. Ordered by severity (paper-critical first).

Legend: **[BLOCKER]** = must fix before the claim can be published ·
**[STRENGTHEN]** = needed to meet reviewer standards · **[NOTE]** = disclose
honestly in Limitations, no new experiment required.

---

## A. Causal steering at the hiring level

### A1. Broad vs local steering regime — **[BLOCKER]**
Notebook 06 steers hiring with the broad range `[-0.5 … +0.5] × mean_resid_norm`.
The concept-level causality work already found this range saturates and switched
to `[-0.1 … +0.1]`. The 27B null/negative steering result is therefore confounded
with steering in the saturation regime (27B baseline is already P(Yes)=0.76).
**Fix:** re-run hiring steering at both scales in the local regime; only then
interpret slopes. Until done, the 27B "scale dissociation" must be labelled
*preliminary / pending robustness check* in the draft.

### A2. Single random-direction null — **[STRENGTHEN]**
Steering uses one random orthogonal direction as control. One sample is not a
null distribution; a random direction can move the margin by chance.
**Fix:** use 20–50 random directions (matched norm) and report the steering effect
as a z-score / percentile against that ensemble, exactly as done for the probe
Cohen's d null.

### A3. Small held-out set — **[NOTE]**
10 held-out topics for concept-level causal tests. Bootstrap CIs are reported, but
coverage of social contexts is limited. Disclose; partly mitigated by the planned
400-story corpus.

## B. The callback signal itself

### B1. Callback margins are quantised to 0.125 — **[BLOCKER for R4]**
Both 12B and 27B callback margins fall on a 0.125 grid (e.g. −0.625, −0.500,
−0.375 …). A single `logit(Yes) − logit(No)` subtraction should be continuous;
landing on 1/8 steps indicates **bf16 precision loss** at the logit magnitudes
involved. The demographic disparity signal R4 needs is *small*, and this
quantisation can be as large as the effect we want to detect.
**Fix:** recompute logits/margins in **float32** (cast the final logits before the
subtraction). Re-verify margin variance increases and the grid disappears.

### B2. Compressed 12B callback variance — **[STRENGTHEN]**
At 12B the callback margin has SD≈0.14 and range −0.625…+0.125: the model says
"No" to almost everyone with little name-to-name variation. Detecting demographic
disparities at 12B may be **underpowered**. At 27B variance is larger (SD≈0.41) but
steering saturates (A1).
**Fix:** (i) fix B1 first — some of the compression is quantisation; (ii) consider
a prompt/profile calibrated so the baseline sits nearer P(Yes)=0.5 with real
spread; (iii) report which model gives the usable disparity signal and justify the
primary-model choice on that basis.

### B3. Name-activation probe context is one fixed template — **[STRENGTHEN]**
Notebook 07 reads a name's internal warmth/competence from one sentence
("The job applicant's name is X.") mean-pooled over non-BOS tokens. The
probe→human correlation (ρ=0.355/0.230) is conditional on this template.
**Fix:** compute the probe score over 3–5 neutral templates and report mean ±
spread; show the correlation is template-robust, not an artifact of one phrasing.

## C. Stimuli and representations

### C1. AI-generated stimuli circularity — **[STRENGTHEN]**
Concept stories were generated by Claude; probes run on Gemma/Qwen/Llama.
Cross-organisation, but still model-authored text — stylistic regularities could
inflate probe accuracy across model families.
**Fix:** validate the directions against an **externally authored** stimulus set.
A sentence-level human-annotated warmth/competence dataset now exists
(arXiv:2601.06316) and can serve as a held-out, non-AI validation of the probe.
Report probe accuracy on it as a circularity control.

### C2. Saturated CV cannot rank models or layers — **[NOTE]**
5-fold and topic-holdout CV are 100% everywhere. This proves linear separability
but is a ceiling, not a precision measure; do not present 100% CV as the *strength*
of the result. Cohen's d (and the random-direction z) are the discriminative
metrics. Already handled in the reports — keep the paper consistent.

### C3. Single seed — **[STRENGTHEN]**
All results use seed 20260527. Variability across seeds is unmeasured.
**Fix:** re-run the headline numbers (probe CV, Cohen's d, steering slopes) for
3–5 seeds and report mean ± SD, at least for the primary model.

### C4. Mean-pooled activations vs token-level SAE features — **[NOTE]**
Gemma Scope SAEs are trained token-level; our story vectors are mean-pooled.
Reconstruction is excellent but feature selection from a pooled vector may differ
from token-wise selection. Already disclosed in the Gemma Scope report; carry the
caveat into the paper.

## D. Statistical reporting

### D1. Multiple comparisons — **[STRENGTHEN]**
Many correlations/tests are reported (probe×human, callback×predictor, per axis,
per model). Some p-values are marginal (e.g. 0.067, 0.084).
**Fix:** state the family of tests and apply a correction (Holm/BH) for the
confirmatory ones, or clearly label marginal results as exploratory.

### D2. Moderate correlations stated carefully — **[NOTE]**
Probe→human ρ=0.355/0.230 (12B), 0.381/0.283 (27B) are moderate. Frame as "the
probe captures a *component* of human social perception," never as faithful
replication. Already phrased this way in the current draft — keep it.

### D3. History of a withdrawn claim — **[NOTE]**
The "cross-axis paradox" was published internally then withdrawn (a scikit-learn
1.9.0 unscaled-LR artifact). The corrected values (0.87/0.82) are in the draft.
Lesson for the audit: keep the fold-local standardisation in any re-run; do not
resurrect the paradox framing.

## E. Scope / external validity (the profession point)

### E1. Single profession, single template — **[STRENGTHEN]**
All hiring results use one role (Administrative Assistant) and one CV. Findings may
be role-specific. This is exactly what the profession extension addresses; see
`docs/profession_extension.md`. At minimum, disclose as a Limitation; ideally run
the lightweight multi-profession design.
